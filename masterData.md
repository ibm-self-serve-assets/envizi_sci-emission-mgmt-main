## 2.1 Setting Up Products
Product data lists everything your organization buys—goods and services. This is essential for tracking emissions, managing suppliers, and making better business decisions in Envizi SCI.

**Why is it important?**
- Helps track spending and supplier performance
- Makes it easy to update as your purchases change
- Connects products to suppliers, locations, and transactions
- Lets you calculate emissions for each product or service or product category wise

### 2.1.1 Get the Product Template

1. Copy the template file with the name *Envizi-SCI-master-Product.csv* 

<div align="center">
  <img src="Images/SCI-master-product-template.png">
</div>

2. Review the header columns and sample data. Detailed explanations for each column are provided [here](https://www.ibm.com/docs/en/envizi-supply-chain?topic=configuring-setting-up-products):


- **Product ID** (Required)
  - Unique identifier for the product. Use an ID generated by your organisation or an existing ERP/ERP‑exported ID.
  - Example: `1001`

- **Supplier ID** (Required)
  - Unique identifier for the supplier. This can be organisation‑generated or taken from your ERP.
  - Example: `Apple`

- **Supplier Product ID** (Optional)
  - Identifier provided by the supplier (product / part number). The `Product ID` is the organisation's unique key.
  - Example: `Apple Macbook Pro 15 inch`

The category codes (L1–L3) create a product hierarchy for classification and reporting. For INBank we use the hierarchy (`L1 > L2 > L3`) for example:  `IT infrastructure > Laptops and Desktops > Computers`). Please refer the [`Product Mapping` template section](productMapping.md) to understand and identify the corresponding `Category ID` for each product. 

- **Category L1 ID** (Required)
  - Top-level category (product family).
  - Example: `IT infrastructure`

- **Category L2 ID** (Required)
  - Mid-level category (group/brand).
  - Example: `Laptops and Desktops`

- **Category L3 ID** (Required)
  - Lowest-level category (specific category).
  - Example: `Computers`

- **Name** (Required)
  - Product name shown in the UI.
  - Example: `Apple MacBook Pro 15"`

- **Description** (Required)
  - Brief free-text description of the product.

- **Default Quantity Units** (Recommended)
  - Recommended: `EA` (Each). Use standard UOM or ISO format where possible.

- **Value** (Required)
  - Price or value of the product (numeric).
  - Example: `1200`

- **Currency** (Required)
  - Currency in ISO 4217 format.
  - Example: `USD`
  - _Note: At the time of writing this document only `USD` is supported._

- **Measurement** (Optional)
  - Numeric measurement used for average‑data emission calculations (becomes required when using that method).
  - Example: `1`

- **Measurement Unit** (Optional)
  - Unit for the measurement value (e.g., `kg`).
  - Example: `kg`

- **Status** (Optional)
  - Useful values: `ACTIVE`, `INACTIVE`.
  - Example: `ACTIVE`

### 2.1.3 Uploading the Products Data to SCI

Follow these steps to upload your completed product master data file into Envizi SCI:

1. **Log in to SCI**
  - Ensure you are in the correct tenant. (Example tenants: `GSI Demos`, `Supply Chain ESG`—your tenant name may differ.)
  <div align="center">
    <img src="Images/SCI-navigation-DM.png" alt="SCI Navigation">
  </div>

2. **Navigate to Data Management**
  - On the left sidebar, go to `Admin > Data Management`.

3. **Start a New Import Job**
  - On the Data Management page, click `Import Data`.
  - In the New Import Job pop-up, click `Add file` and select your completed product master data file (e.g., `Envizi-SCI-master-Product_INBank.csv`).
  - You can leave `Ingest priority` as default.

4. **Select Data Mapping**
  - From the list of Data Mappings, select `Master data - Product (masterDataProduct)`.
  - Leave `Data mapping version` and `Delimiter` as default (`COMMA`).

5. **Set Other Import job Options**

  - `Object types` will be auto-populated based on your mapping. (The Product template creates two objects: `Product` and `Product Supplier`.)
  - Leave `Bucket` as default: `Intelligence Suite default file storage`.
  
<div align="center">
  <img src="Images/Image6.png" alt="Data Mapping Selection">
</div>
  
6. **Review and Import**
  - Click `Next` to review your selections.
  
  <div align="center">
  <img src="Images/SCI-DM-import-Prod-review.png" alt="Data Mapping Selection">
</div>
  


  - Click `Import` to start the job.
  - You should see a message: _New import job created. Job {Job ID} has been successfully created._

7. **Check Import Status**
  - Uploading the product master data will create two jobs (for the two object types) - `Product` and `Product Supplier`. [Review data model](Images/SCI-datamodel-master-2.png)
  
<div align="center">
      <img src="Images/SCI-DM-import-Prod-review.png" alt="Import Review Status">
</div>
  
  Ensure both import jobs have completed successfully before proceeding.

  <div align="center">
    <img src="Images/SCI-DM-import-Prod-job.png" alt="Product jobs">
  </div>

  If a job is `CompletedWithErrors` or `Rejected`:
  - Open the job details page and review the error messages.
  - See the Troubleshooting guide: [Troubleshooting](troubleshooting.md).
  - Identify the root cause, correct the CSV, and re-run the import.
  - Repeat until both jobs show `Completed`.

8. **View Products from Data Explorer**
  - Navigate to  `Reporting > Data Exploer > Products` to view the products.

- To consolidate and report emissions at organization level
- To track total number of suppliers the organization involved with
- To track total number of products the organiztionation is purchasing
- Get view on the procurement sites as well as source countries.
- To ensure reporting matches the real company structure

## 2.4 Setting up Suppliers

Supplier data lists all the companies or people the organization buys goods and services from. Keeping this information up to date is key for accurate emissions tracking, supplier engagement, and better business decisions in Envizi SCI.

**Why is it important?**
- To calculate emissions for each supplier
- Links suppliers to products, locations, and transactions
- To engage with suppliers to capture supplier-specific data such as 
  - Product carbon footprint, ESG survey
  - ESG Risk, Net-zero targets
  - Supplier due diligence
  - Carbon-offset programs, etc
- Powers detailed supplier reports and dashboards
